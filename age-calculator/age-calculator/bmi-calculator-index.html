Understanding BMI

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a value derived from the mass (weight) and height of a person. It is a simple tool used to categorize individuals as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. It helps give a general idea of whether you have a healthy body weight for your height.

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is a useful indicator, it does not distinguish between fat and muscle mass. Therefore, muscular individuals (like athletes) may have a high BMI but low body fat. It's best used as one of several tools to assess health, not as a sole diagnostic measure.
